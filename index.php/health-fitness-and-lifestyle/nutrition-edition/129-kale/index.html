Kale

Written on 12 July 2017.

Kale is one of the most nutrient leafy vegetables to be consumed these days. Besides its rich green color, Kale is full of minerals and vitamins (Vit A, C, E and K), is low in calories, fat free and has phytochemicals called lutein and zeaxanthin.

Kale has been linked with a low ris

k of cancer, osteoporosis, eyes and heart diseases. Kale is enriched with Vit K that helps to maintain strong bones especially in women who are more at risk to bone degeneration.

kale
Kale is easily available at the grocery stores or at farmers market with other greens and the best time to buy kale is during winter or early spring. Look for a bunch of kale that is deeply colored and has firm and hardy stems. Avoid picking kale that is yellow, brown or wilted.
To store kale, try wrapping it in a moist paper towel/cloth to increase its shelf life. Unwashed Kale can also be stored in a crisper in the fridge for up to 1 week. Usually the flavors of kale become stronger after 5 days so try and consume it before that.

Fat 0.3 g
Protein 1.3 g
Carbohydrate 4 g

Retinol 468 mg
Vitamin C 28 mg
Vitamin K 531 mcg
Potassium 157 mg

Folate 9 mcg
Iron 0.6 mg
Lutein 12 mg
