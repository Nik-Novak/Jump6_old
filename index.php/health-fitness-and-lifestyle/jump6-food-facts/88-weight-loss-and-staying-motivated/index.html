Weight loss and staying motivated

 When it comes to weight loss do you sometimes feel like you have 2 personalities?

 

Fat guy V.S Skinny guy??  Like for instance you become skinny guy on Sunday vowing to start the week off right, eat well, go to the gym, you clean out your fridge and rid your home of carbs, sugary snacks and go to bed motivated and ready for the week ahead, determined to see a difference.  Monday you are skinny guy...you eat breakfast consisting of eggs and protein..no toast! Or maybe you blended a green smoothie in an effort to really start this weight loss thing!  Your doing great...day 1 and things are going great. Tuesday you are still skinny guy, you skipped out on meeting friends for dinner and drinks, you ate at home and feel good about your decisions today. 

Now we are on Wednesday and fat guy is creeping in...your craving carbs and sugar of course and you did not prepare lunch today...fat guy and skinny guy are battling….”go for the salad says skinny guy” Fat guy really wants that sub , loaded with meat and delicious homemade baked bread….its too much for skinny guy to beat and fat guy takes over just 3 days in, now you find yourself devouring the delicious sub you could not refuse….now riddled with guilt you seem to forget skinny guys presence for the last 3 days and continue on with fat guy because its well….easier...comfortable...familiar.  See weight loss is such a battle in your mind and what keeps one motivated to continue to forget about fat guy and stay with skinny guy.  Often we want instant gratification which with weight loss doesn’t happen, it takes time, it takes dedication, and motivation.  It’s easy to become depressed and hopeless and just give up before 1 week of eating better.

It’s okay to struggle and its okay to fail but its how you come back from it.  If you quit then quit, be happy with your health and your size and move on.  However if you are determined and truly want to see a difference in your health and the scale then stick with it.  It may take a few months but there is nothing better  then slipping on some jeans that you feel good in again or when friends start to notice that you look great and are seeing a difference.  Your gaining your life back and confidence and that feels truly amazing!  There is no magic to weight loss, its hard work, dedication, and staying focused t meet your goal.  Nothing in life is easy and there is no exception to weight loss.  Find what makes it fun for you to lose weight such as  exercise, cooking healthy meals, walking ,taking the stairs instead of the elevator...a little work  each day into your health goes a long way and if you continue on that path you will find success.  Stick with it.!!
